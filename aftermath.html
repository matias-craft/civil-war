Reconstruction Era

The period following the Civil War from 1865 to 1877, focused on rebuilding the South and integrating formerly enslaved people into society.

13th Amendment

Ratified in 1865, this amendment abolished slavery in the United States.

Economic Impact

The war devastated the Southern economy, leading to a long period of economic hardship and transformation in the region.

Social Changes

The war led to significant social changes, including shifts in gender roles and the beginning of the civil rights movement for African Americans.
