Major Battles of the American Civil War

Battle of Gettysburg

The Battle of Gettysburg (July 1-3, 1863) was a turning point in the Civil War, resulting in a significant Union victory and halting the Confederate invasion of the North.

Battle of Antietam

The Battle of Antietam (September 17, 1862) is known as the bloodiest single-day battle in American history, with over 22,000 casualties. It ended in a tactical draw but provided President Lincoln the opportunity to issue the Emancipation Proclamation.

Battle of Fort Sumter

The Battle of Fort Sumter (April 12-13, 1861) marked the beginning of the Civil War when Confederate forces fired on the Union garrison, leading to its surrender.
